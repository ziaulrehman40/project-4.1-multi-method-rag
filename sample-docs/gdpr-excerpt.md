# Data Protection Policy (Illustrative Excerpt)

*This is a simplified, illustrative excerpt written for a training exercise. It is not the official text of the GDPR.*

## Data Protection Principles

Personal data must be handled according to six principles:

1. **Lawfulness, fairness, and transparency** — processed lawfully and in a way people can understand.
2. **Purpose limitation** — collected for specified, explicit purposes and not used in incompatible ways.
3. **Data minimisation** — limited to what is necessary for those purposes.
4. **Accuracy** — kept accurate and up to date.
5. **Storage limitation** — kept in identifiable form no longer than necessary.
6. **Integrity and confidentiality** — protected against unauthorised access, loss, or damage.

## Lawful Basis for Processing

At least one lawful basis must apply before processing personal data: consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, or legitimate interests. The basis must be identified and recorded before processing begins.

## Rights of the Data Subject

Individuals have the right to be informed, the right of access, the right to rectification, the right to erasure, the right to restrict processing, the right to data portability, and the right to object. A request must be answered without undue delay and within one month.

## Personal Data Breaches

A personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to individuals must be reported to the supervisory authority without undue delay, and no later than 72 hours after becoming aware of it. Where the risk is high, affected individuals must also be informed.
